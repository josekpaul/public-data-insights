Party-unity rate

share of contested votes with own party majority

Across 110 contested House recorded votes this Congress, all five Ohio Democrats voted with their party at least 99% of the time. Ohio Republicans show a wider spread — Warren Davidson and David Joyce break ranks most often, while Jim Jordan and David Taylor stay close to leadership.

Stock trading activity

Periodic Transaction Reports, this Congress

Two members account for nearly all disclosed trading in Ohio's delegation: David Taylor (R-2) filed 199 transactions and Greg Landsman (D-1) filed 56. Both trade across a broad, diversified basket of large-cap names rather than concentrated bets — Taylor's trades top out around $50,000 each, so his roughly $3.3M cumulative disclosed range comes from volume, not size. The other 13 members disclosed at most a handful of trades each; ten disclosed none.

Campaign finance

current cycle, as of latest FEC filing

Individual-donor share varies more than three-fold across the delegation. Jim Jordan and Emilia Sykes raise most heavily from individual contributors; Michael Rulli, Mike Carey, and Warren Davidson lean more on PAC and party-committee money. Fundraising totals mostly track seat competitiveness rather than seniority — several longer-tenured members have modest war chests because their seats aren't seriously contested.

Methodology. Voting data covers House recorded votes only, using the same 110-vote contested-vote sample as our House GOP party-unity brief; "party-unity rate" is 100% minus each member's crossover rate. Stock-trade data is parsed from Periodic Transaction Report PDFs filed with the Clerk of the House and covers transactions reported in calendar years 2025–2026; extraction is regex-based against the PDF's text layer and a small number of asset-name labels may still contain minor artifacts. Campaign finance figures are FEC candidate committee totals for the current election cycle, pulled via the FEC's /candidates and /committees/totals endpoints. Senators Bernie Moreno and Jon Husted are not included — Senate financial disclosures are filed through a separate system (efdsearch.senate.gov) that wasn't accessible via this report's data pipeline.

This is a first-pass exploratory read, not a definitive study. Zero disclosed trades does not necessarily mean zero investment activity — it may reflect assets held in unreportable vehicles (e.g. certain trusts) or simply no covered transactions during this period.
